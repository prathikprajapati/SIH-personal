Frequently Asked Questions

How does SecureErase ensure data is completely destroyed?
Our proprietary multi-pass wiping algorithm follows military-grade standards including DoD 5220.22-M and NIST SP 800-88 guidelines. We use advanced cryptographic erase techniques that overwrite data multiple times with random patterns, making recovery impossible even with advanced forensic tools.
Is my data safe during the erasure process?
Absolutely. Your data never leaves your device during the erasure process. Our solution operates entirely offline and uses hardware-level operations to ensure complete data destruction without any network transmission. All processes are verified and logged for your peace of mind.
What types of devices do you support?
We support all major storage devices including HDDs, SSDs, NVMe drives, USB flash drives, SD cards, and mobile devices. Our system automatically detects and handles different device types with appropriate wiping algorithms for each format.
Do you provide certificates for compliance?
Yes, every erasure process generates a detailed certificate with cryptographic proof of destruction, timestamp, device information, and verification hashes. These certificates meet regulatory requirements for data destruction and can be exported in PDF and JSON formats.
How long does the erasure process take?
Processing time varies by device size and type. Small USB drives typically complete in under 30 seconds, while larger hard drives may take 5-15 minutes. Our system provides real-time progress updates and estimates based on your specific device.
What happens if I interrupt the process?
Our system is designed to handle interruptions gracefully. If interrupted, the process will resume from where it left off when you restart. However, we recommend completing the process uninterrupted for optimal results and to avoid potential data inconsistencies.
